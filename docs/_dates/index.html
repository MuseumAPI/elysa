Date Information

Provenance timespans are by their nature imprecise. Rarely if ever is a transfer of ownership known to the minute, and often the uncertainty is much, much greater than that. As such, when representing a date, we have three pieces of information. The date itself, the precision of the date, and the certainty of the date.

For precision, we have Century, Decade, Year, Month, Day, and None. This selection of ranges captures most of the variance seen in actual provenance records.

Currently, we do not have a technical solution for representing dates such as "The early 1900s". Our suggestion for such dates would be to use an earliest/latest range, such as "Sometime between 1900 and 1950".

For certainty, we have Certain and Uncertain. It would be possilbe to represent varying levels of uncertainty by using phrases such as "Possibly", "Probably", and "Likely", but without a strong, controlled vocabulary describing the differences between them, an external reader will assume all of them are merely a marker of uncertainty in general. Since we lack this vocabulary, we have chosen to treat certainty as a boolean flag.

Dates are represented as internally be the earliest date within the range dictated by the precision of the date.

For example, 1995 is represented as January 1, 1995, with a precision of YEAR, and October 2000 is represented by October 1, 2000 with a precision of MONTH.
